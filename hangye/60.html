Industry

Starlink accessories by industry and use case.

Marine & Boating Communications

For Starlink users who have the most demanding equipment requirements, peripheral accessories are even more crucial. The marine environment is extremely harsh, with high temperatures, high humidity, and high corrosiveness, placing higher demands on the material selection and structural design of related accessories. Therefore, we will be dedicated to developing a full range of cables, mounting brackets, protective housings, and other products specifically for these users to ensure uninterrupted network signal coverage during long-distance ocean voyages.

 

Pain points

Severe shaking and corrosion: Continuous swaying and rolling of the hull affect antenna stability for satellite alignment; the high salinity and humidity environment corrodes equipment casings and interfaces.

Non-standard power supply requirements: Ships generally use 12V/24V DC power systems, which are incompatible with the original power adapters.

Cable and interface safety: Vibration can easily cause connections to loosen; seawater corrosion may cause short circuits.

 

Demand points

High-strength marine antenna mounting bracket (anti-sway), dedicated DC power converter (12V/24V to 48V PoE), waterproof and corrosion-resistant interface protective sleeve, and dedicated thickened Ethernet cable.
