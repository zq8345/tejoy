System Integration & Deployment

From installation, power supply, signal transmission, port adaptation, and storage, we provide a complete solution for Starlink device peripherals and accessories. Whether it's a fixed installation, a temporary network setup, or a special application scenario, we have the capability to provide a complete one-stop solution and hope to discuss and exchange ideas with you from the perspective of user needs.

 

Pain points

● Compatibility issues include: Difficulty in identifying compatibility issues: The electrical and physical compatibility of different brands of components (brackets, power supplies, cables) with Starlink terminals needs to be researched independently, resulting in high trial-and-error costs.

● Lack of environmental adaptation solutions: No ready-made integrated solutions exist for specific scenarios (e.g., high-vibration trucks, highly corrosive ship cabins), requiring in-house design and testing, which is time-consuming and labor-intensive.

● Non-standardized installation process: The lack of unified installation specifications, wiring guidelines, and debugging checklists increases the difficulty of on-site deployment and the failure rate. Coordination with multiple suppliers: Components need to be procured from multiple suppliers separately, involving communication, price comparison, and logistics coordination, resulting in extremely high management costs.

● Inconsistent quality and standards: The quality of components from different suppliers varies, affecting the overall system reliability and making it difficult to define after-sales responsibility.

● Inconsistent delivery times: Inconsistent delivery times for different components cause delays in the project's critical path.

● Multiple technical support providers: When problems arise, multiple suppliers need to be contacted for troubleshooting, leading to low communication efficiency and a tendency for blame-shifting.

● Inconsistent warranty periods: Different components have different warranty periods, making it difficult to provide end customers with a unified service commitment.

● Lack of overall accountability: No single supplier is responsible for the final performance of the entire integrated system.

 

Demand points

● Introducing "Scenario-Based Kits": Combining verified and compatible accessories into ready-to-use kits (such as "All-Purpose RV Long-Distance Travel Kits" and "Marine Basic Installation Kits"), accompanied by detailed installation videos and Bills of Materials (BOM).

● Providing "Solution Consulting and Customization" Services: Offering technical solution design and customized product development services for large or special projects.

● Declaring a "Unified After-Sales Commitment": Providing a comprehensive warranty for core kits and committing to providing technical support from a single point of contact for your team.

● Core Value: Freeing you from tedious "integration work" so you can focus on your core business. Improving product reliability and supply chain management efficiency.
