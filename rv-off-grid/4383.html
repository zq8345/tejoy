1. Why Starlink Mini Is Perfect for RV Camping

The Starlink Mini is the ideal satellite internet solution for RV and camping enthusiasts. Its compact size (29 x 25 cm, just 2.9 kg) and low power consumption (20-40W) make it uniquely suited for mobile and off-grid use.

With the right Tejoy accessories, you can have reliable internet anywhere you park — whether you are at a full-hookup RV park, a remote boondocking site, or a beachside campsite.

3. Step 1: Choose Your Mounting Location

Once you have parked your RV, walk around and find the spot with the clearest view of the sky. Use the Starlink app's "Field of View" tool to check for obstructions. Key guidelines:

  • Minimum 100-degree clear view of the sky
  • Avoid tree cover — even bare branches in winter can cause interruptions
  • Mount as high as possible — elevated positions have fewer obstructions
  • Consider nearby structures — buildings, hills, and other RVs can block the signal

4. Step 2: Mount the Terminal

  1. Attach the Pipe Mount to your chosen location (ladder, railing, or rack)
  2. Thread the U-bolts around the structure and tighten by hand
  3. Snug the bolts with a wrench — but do not fully tighten yet
  4. Attach the adapter plate to the mount bracket
  5. Place the Starlink Mini onto the adapter plate and secure with the provided bolts
  6. Tilt the Mini to approximately 25-35 degrees upward (for northern hemisphere)
  7. Fully tighten all bolts using a cross pattern for even torque
Important: Do not overtighten the terminal mounting bolts. The Starlink Mini has a plastic casing. Hand-tighten plus a quarter turn with a wrench is sufficient.

5. Step 3: Connect Power

The Starlink Mini is unique among Starlink terminals because it natively supports 12V DC power (no inverter needed). The Tejoy 12V Power Adapter allows you to power the Mini directly from:

  • Your RV's house battery (most RV battery systems run at 12V)
  • A portable power station (Jackery, Bluetti, or similar with 12V DC output)
  • A car cigarette lighter port (via the Tejoy Car Adapter accessory)
  • A solar-powered battery system (see our solar setup guide)

Connection Steps

  1. Connect the Tejoy 12V Adapter to your 12V power source (observe polarity: red = positive, black = negative)
  2. Plug the Starlink Mini power cable into the adapter
  3. The Mini will power on automatically and begin searching for satellites
  4. Typical boot time: 2-5 minutes (faster after the first setup)
Using the Tejoy 12V Adapter instead of the stock AC power supply saves 10-20% power by eliminating inverter losses. For a typical 8-hour camping day, this can save 30-60 Wh of battery capacity.

6. Step 4: Route and Secure Cables

Proper cable management prevents tripping hazards and protects your equipment:

  1. Route the power cable from the mount location along the RV to your power source entry point
  2. Use UV cable ties to secure the cable every 30 cm (12 inches)
  3. Create a drip loop at the mount end — a U-shaped curve below the connection point prevents water from following the cable into the connector
  4. Pass the cable inside through a window gap using the Flat Cable Adapter, or through an existing exterior access point
  5. Do not pinch the cable in windows or doors — this damages the insulation over time

7. Step 5: Activate and Test

  1. Open the Starlink app on your phone
  2. Log in to your Starlink account and activate the Roam plan if you haven't already
  3. Check the app for "Obstructions: None" — if you see "Obstructed," try a different location or adjust the mount height
  4. Run a speed test — expect 50-150 Mbps download, 10-20 Mbps upload
  5. Check power draw — the app shows current power consumption (typically 25-35W during use)

8. Camping Pro Tips

Before You Leave

  • Test your full setup at home before your first trip
  • Pre-install the Starlink app and activate your account
  • Charge all portable batteries to 100%
  • Pack your Tejoy accessories in a dedicated tote
  • Download offline maps of your camping area

At the Campsite

  • Set up before sunset — much easier in daylight
  • Check the Starlink app's obstruction tool before tightening the mount
  • If trees are a problem, move the RV 10 feet — it can make the difference
  • In windy conditions, lower the mount height or add guy lines
  • Bring a backup power source (even a small 12V battery pack)
